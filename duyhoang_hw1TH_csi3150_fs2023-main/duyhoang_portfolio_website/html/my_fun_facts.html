Favorite Book

American Prometheus is a biography of J. Robert Oppenheimer, the scientist who led the Manhattan Project to develop the atomic bomb. The book explores his life, his achievements, his struggles, and his legacy. It also examines the moral and political implications of his work, and how he faced the consequences of scientific progress. The title refers to the Greek myth of Prometheus, who stole fire from the gods and was punished for it.
